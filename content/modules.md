A **module** represents a functional unit of logic and behavior that lives within your [[personal]] [[computing]] [[network]], rather than being siloed inside a traditional [[app]]. By shifting our focus from shipping rigid software containers to orchestrating interconnected modules, we reclaim [[sovereignty]] over our digital [[environment]]. These modules float seamlessly between devices, ensuring [[interoperability]] and state synchronization; a notification or a camera feed becomes an extension of your own system rather than a dependency on an external vendor. 

> [!idea] 
> Modules allow us to treat our devices as parts of a unified fabric, where adding hardware—like a camera—simply introduces its native module into your existing workflow.

Ultimately, this architectural shift facilitates a more [[malleable]] relationship with the tools we use, turning computing into an extension of personal intent rather than a collection of fractured experiences.
