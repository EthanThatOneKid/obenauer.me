[[linking]] is the fundamental act of externalizing the relationships within our [[cognition]] by choosing to ==declare that association== between distinct [[items]]. By bridging these gaps, we move away from isolated silos and toward a fluid, interconnected [[environment]] where our [[thinking]] is not limited by the artificial boundaries of software. It is through this deliberate gesture that we transform disparate data into a meaningful [[graph]], granting us greater [[agency]] over the digital landscape we inhabit.

> [!note] Intentionality
> Linking is not merely metadata; it is the manifestation of human intent within a machine. It converts passive storage into active, searchable knowledge.

> [!idea] Future
> When we prioritize linking, we prioritize the connections between ideas over the containers that hold them.
