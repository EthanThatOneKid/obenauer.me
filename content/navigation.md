[[navigation]] is the foundational act of moving through our digital
environments, reimagined as a fluid exploration of [[paths]], [[context]], and
the underlying [[graph]] of our personal knowledge. Rather than rigid
hierarchies, [[navigation]] relies on ==browsing active [[context]]== and
tracking recent journeys, allowing us to maintain [[agency]] as we move between
[[items]] and their varied [[relationships]]. By prioritizing semantic movement
over mechanical file traversal, we transform the [[interfaces]] from a container
into a living, responsive space.

> [!idea] True [[navigation]] is not about finding a location, but about
> sustaining the flow of thought while shifting between different dimensions of
> data.

> [!note] Our digital [[tools|tools]] should support our natural tendency to traverse
> information through [[relationships]] rather than strictly predefined
> [[paths]].
