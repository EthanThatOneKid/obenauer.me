# interoperability

[[interoperability]] is the antitoxin to digital silos, moving beyond mere file formats to the fundamental ability for systems and data to truly collaborate. It serves as a core enabler for [[computing]], where your data acts as a fluid resource that flows, transforms, and combines across [[software]] environments without friction. By rejecting the isolation inherent in traditional [[interface]] design, we regain agency, ensuring that users—not vendors—dictate the flow of information and the construction of their digital worlds.

> [!important]
> When tools interoperate, your data is no longer imprisoned; it becomes a composable, intelligent workspace, allowing for genuine cognitive augmentation.

> [!idea]
> We must transition from isolated windows to a system where data items, accompanied by views from their hosting apps, can be moved freely across our digital landscapes.
