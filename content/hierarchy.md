# hierarchy

In our current digital environments, hierarchy is often treated as a rigid, static container, yet it should instead function as a fluid layer of [[organization]] that supports [[navigation]] through complex [[corpus]]. By moving away from brittle, forced structures and embracing [[itemized]] relationships, we can redefine how we browse our [[workspace]]. True [[cognition]] in our tools requires that the way we view our [[items]] mirrors the organic way we think, allowing for flexible [[views]] that transcend the limitations of traditional file-system silos.

> [!idea] 
> We must move beyond the constraints of fixed nesting. Hierarchy should be a soft, malleable association between units of information, not a cage that restricts their utility or visibility.

By leveraging [[semantics]] and [[linking]], we can surface depth where it is needed without sacrificing the agility required for daily [[workflow]]. We are reimagining the [[os]] not as a map of folders, but as a dynamic landscape where the user’s intent defines the depth of their focus.
