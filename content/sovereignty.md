[[sovereignty]] is the bedrock of personal [[computing]], representing a fundamental shift in power where your information—your thoughts, your work, your life’s traces—resides exclusively under your control. Most modern [[software]] treats us as digital serfs, where we are tenants in a landlord’s building. To be truly sovereign, your digital existence must be [[portability|portable]], [[malleability|mutable]], and yours to command, free from the capricious whims of distant servers or corporate agendas. Without this control, we cannot cultivate the personal [[environment]] necessary to support our own unique ways of [[thinking]].

> [!important]
> Data sovereignty means your information is not merely accessible; it is yours to command.

When we reclaim this agency, we move beyond the general-case interfaces that force us to contort our cognition, allowing us instead to design digital realms that reflect our specific perspectives and needs.
