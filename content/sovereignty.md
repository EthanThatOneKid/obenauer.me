# [[sovereignty]]

Most current software functions like a landlord-tenant relationship: you reside
within a building governed by someone else's rules, subject to their eviction.
True [[sovereignty]] flips this power dynamic entirely. It posits that your
digital traces—your thoughts, your work, and the artifacts of your life—are
unequivocally yours, not merely accessible but inherently [[local]] and yours to
command.

> [!important] When data is truly sovereign, it is ==mutable, portable, and
> under your absolute control==.

This is the bedrock for [[computing]], liberating the individual from the
capricious whims of corporate agendas and distant servers. By anchoring our
digital existence in [[agency]] and [[programming]], we move away from being
digital serfs toward becoming architects of our own [[augmentation]]
environments. Without this autonomy, any attempt at genuine personal
[[computing]] remains an illusion.
