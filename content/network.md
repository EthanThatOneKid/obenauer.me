> [!important]
> A **network** is your own personal, internet-connected architecture of private [[devices]] and [[facts]]. It transcends traditional silos, acting as the foundation for [[sovereignty]] where your [[items]]—composed of [[views]] and [[automations]]—exist with fluidity. By enabling automatic, redundant duplication of information across your hardware, it ensures [[localfirst]] access while remaining open to purposeful, controlled interactions with the broader world.

By treating the network as a unified, extensible environment, we move beyond the limitations of static machines. It is the connective tissue that allows us to integrate diverse hardware—from laptops to ambient sensors—into a cohesive system that reflects our specific [[intent]]. This shift toward a modular, [[items|item-centric]] approach empowers us to regain control over our digital lives, fostering an ecosystem where [[computing]] is an extension of our own thinking and [[workflow]] rather than a barrier to it.
