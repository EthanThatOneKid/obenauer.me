> [!important]
> A **network** is your own [[personal]], [[internet]]-connected [[architecture]] of private [[device|devices]] and [[fact|facts]]. It transcends traditional [[silo|silos]], acting as the foundation for [[sovereignty]] where your [[item|items]]—composed of [[view|views]] and [[automation|automations]]—exist with fluidity.
 By enabling automatic, redundant duplication of information across your hardware, it ensures [[localfirst]] access while remaining open to purposeful, controlled [[interaction|interactions]] with the broader world.

By treating the network as a unified, extensible [[environment]], we move beyond the limitations of static machines. It is the connective tissue that allows us to integrate diverse hardware—from laptops to ambient sensors—into a cohesive [[system]] that reflects our specific [[intent]]. This shift toward a modular, item-centric approach empowers us to regain control over our digital lives,
 fostering an ecosystem where [[computing]] is an extension of our own [[thinking]] and [[workflow]] rather than a barrier to it.
