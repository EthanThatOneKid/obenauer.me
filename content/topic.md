Topics provide a fluid mechanism for structuring our [[workspace]], enabling the
collection of any [[item|items]]—regardless of type—into a meaningful
[[organization]] or [[graph]]. By moving beyond rigid [[silo|silos]], we embrace
a [[system]] where a single entity can inhabit multiple [[context|contexts]]
simultaneously, fostering organic [[discovery]] and intuitive [[organization]].

> [!idea] The power of topics lies in their flexibility; they allow us to pin
> [[reference|references]] and cultivate [[domain|domains]] of thought that
> evolve alongside our [[project|projects]].

By leveraging these non-exclusive groupings, we achieve a more human-centric
[[architecture]] of information, turning static data into active, interconnected
[[knowledge]]. This promotes true [[sovereignty]] over how we relate to our
digital [[asset|assets]].
