To understand the future of [[computing]], we must first interrogate the ==fundamental units of personal computing==. These are the atomic building blocks of our digital existence—the discrete packets of thought and information we [[externalization|externalize]] into the machine. By shifting toward an [[itemcentric]] architecture, we move away from monolithic silos and toward a landscape where these units allow for fluid [[composition]], enabling us to represent our complex, interior lives with greater fidelity and agency.
