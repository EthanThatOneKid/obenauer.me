# localfirst

[[localfirst]] is not merely a technical constraint; it is a profound philosophical shift, reimagining the relationship between the human and the machine by prioritizing [[sovereignty]] and [[symbiosis]]. By anchoring data and computation directly on the user's device, we move away from the fragility of remote server farms, fostering a [[computing]] environment that is inherently resilient and faster. This shift empowers the individual, ensuring that our digital tools belong to us, functioning seamlessly offline while treating the network as an adjunct for synchronization rather than the central authority.

> [!note] The Essence
> ==Local-first design places the locus of control in the hands of the user, creating a fluid workspace where the machine serves the person, not the service provider.==

This approach naturally yields a rich [[timeline]] of our interactions, as every change is owned and recorded locally. It is a necessary evolution toward true personal computing, where our digital tools are as stable and reliable as the physical artifacts in our workspace, ultimately deepening our creative potential.
