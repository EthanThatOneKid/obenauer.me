A ==domain== represents a cohesive sphere of activity or knowledge, acting as the semantic boundaries within which our tools and [[computing]] systems should operate. By anchoring our [[environment]] to meaningful domains rather than arbitrary file structures, we reclaim the [[context]] necessary for genuine [[thinking]].

> [!idea] A domain is the canvas of our cognitive labor.

When we transition from siloed applications to a domain-oriented [[architecture]], we dissolve the friction between our intent and the digital [[assets]] we manipulate. This shift empowers true [[agency]], allowing our software to mirror the fluidity of our own mental maps rather than forcing us into the rigid containers of legacy [[design]].
