A [[workspace]] is an [[item]] within an [[itemcentric]] [[os]], serving as a non-volatile container for heterogenous collections of items. By referencing the items opened within it, a workspace allows you to capture a specific [[context]]—a line of work or a train of thought—to which you can return later.

> [!idea]
> Digital workspaces should be [[malleable]] and fluid rather than rigid, supporting both the continuation of past work and the ability to summarize previous thinking to embark on new, detailed pursuits.

Ultimately, workspaces provide the structure needed to manage complex [[workflow]] by enabling persistent access to the assets and [[paths]] that define our cognitive environments.
