Programs are currently rigid, pre-packaged silos that enforce an external logic onto our digital [[environment]]. We treat them as fixed destinations—places where we are guests rather than owners. True [[computing]] should instead offer the [[malleability]] to redesign, remix, and reconfigure these tools to fit our unique [[workflow]].

> [!important]
> When software is static, it limits our [[sovereignty]]. We must transition from consuming rigid applications to manipulating the [[system]] itself to reflect our own [[intent]].

By moving beyond the current constraints, we can treat the composition of data and views as a singular act of personal [[creation]]. Rather than fighting the interface, we should possess the [[agency]] to mold our digital workspace as naturally as we remodel a physical room.
