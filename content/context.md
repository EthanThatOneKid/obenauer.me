[[context|Context]] is the bedrock of [[agency]], grounding our
[[interaction|interactions]] within a specific, empirical reality rather than
leaving them as abstract concepts. Without a tether to the tangible—be it a
[[document]], an [[environment]], or a specific [[timeline]]—our [[tool|tools]]
fail to support human cognition, [[rendering]] creative potential speculative.
It is through the intentional surfacing of these [[relationship|relationships]]
that we regain the ability to think clearly, transforming isolated data into
meaningful, navigable [[system|systems]].

> [!note] Meaningful action requires grounding. If our [[interface|interfaces]]
> do not provide the full picture of where, why, and how we are working, they
> become distractions rather than extensions of the mind.

> [!idea] By moving toward an [[item]] [[architecture]], we can ensure that
> every piece of information exists within a rich, interconnected web, making
> the experience of [[computing]] feel less like navigating [[silo|silos]] and
> more like a fluid [[process]] of [[thinking]].
