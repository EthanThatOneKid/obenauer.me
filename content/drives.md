[[drives]] represent the foundational mechanisms within our personal [[computing]] environment that bridge the gap between abstract [[services]] and the concrete [[items]] we interact with daily. By decoupling data from the rigid, siloed interfaces of traditional [[apps]], drives allow us to treat information with true [[sovereignty]], enabling us to flow across disparate [[domains]] without friction. This approach transforms our [[workspaces]] into fluid, user-defined environments where every [[asset]]—regardless of its origin—is a first-class citizen, fostering deeper [[cognition]] and more meaningful [[creation]].

> [!idea] The shift from "apps" to "items" mediated by services and drives is essential for achieving a truly malleable and integrated digital life.

> [!important] By moving away from fixed windows and toward a unified [[graph]], we reclaim control over our digital [[corpus]], ensuring that our [[data]] remains a reflection of our own [[intent]] rather than the constraints of a provider.
