[[fact]] represent the fundamental building blocks of our digital environments, acting as the atomic truth against which we navigate our [[thinking]]. By treating data as malleable, queryable elements, we shift from managing opaque files toward a system of [[discovery]] that respects our innate [[curiosity]]. When we decouple these truths from rigid application silos, we create an [[os]] that functions as an extension of our own thinking process rather than a container for vendor-imposed workflows.

> [!important]
> The goal is to evolve from passive consumption to active engagement, where every piece of information exists in a fluid state of [[context]], easily reconnected to other insights to form new knowledge.

Ultimately, facts should be accessible, granular, and inherently portable, allowing the user to construct their own reality rather than being confined by the limitations of traditional software architectures.
