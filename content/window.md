The traditional concept of a [[window|window]]—a container for a self-contained program—is a relic of legacy [[computing]] paradigms like WIMP. By enforcing rigid boundaries, current [[software]] keeps our digital [[item]] siloed and isolated. I envision a future where we transcend this limitation, moving toward [[view]] that allow us to pull data out of these containers to create cohesive, interrelated environments.

> [!idea]
> We must replace the concept of windows as application-centric silos with a model that prioritizes the [[graph]] of related data, allowing items to exist and be manipulated across different [[context]] levels regardless of their origin.
