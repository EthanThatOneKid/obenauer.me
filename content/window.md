The traditional concept of a [[window|window]]—a [[container]] for a self-contained program—is a relic of legacy [[computing]] paradigms like WIMP. By enforcing rigid boundaries, current [[software]] keeps our digital [[item]] siloed and isolated. I envision a [[future]] where we transcend this limitation, moving toward [[view]] that allow us to pull data out of these [[container|containers]] to create cohesive, interrelated [[environment|environments]].

> [!idea]
> We must replace the concept of windows as application-centric [[silo|silos]] with a [[model]] that prioritizes the [[graph]] of related data, allowing [[item|items]] to exist and be manipulated across different [[context]] levels regardless of their origin.
