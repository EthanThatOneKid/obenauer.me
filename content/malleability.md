# malleability

[[malleability]] is the core requirement for true [[computing]], allowing the user to treat their digital [[environment]] as a personal workshop rather than a rigid, predefined space. Current [[interface]] paradigms mistakenly treat software as fixed, static containers owned by developers, but we must shift toward a future where reconfiguring, remodeling, and remixing one's tools is as intuitive as rearranging a room. When software is malleable, it reflects the user’s individual [[thinking]] process, transforming passive consumption into active, creative agency.

> [!idea]
> We must stop asking, "What can I do within this box?" and start asking, "How can I reshape this tool to fit my intent?"

Ultimately, this is about reclaiming the power to modify both data and view, ensuring that our digital tools evolve alongside our needs rather than dictating them.
