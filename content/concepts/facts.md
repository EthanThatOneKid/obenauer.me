# facts

[[facts]] represent the ==fundamental, granular building blocks of our digital
landscape==, acting as the atomic [[units|units]] that underpin [[items]], [[items]]
architectures, and [[environment]]. By shifting our focus from opaque,
monolithic application silos toward these transparent, interoperable data
points, we reclaim [[agency]] and foster a more meaningful [[symbiosis]].

> [!idea] When we treat information as [[facts]] rather than trapped data, we
> enable a future where [[portability]] is the default, allowing our digital
> [[memory]] to flow freely across the [[workspaces]] we inhabit.

Ultimately, defining information as [[facts]] is about restoring the
relationship between human [[intent|intent]] and the computer's output. It moves us away
from passive consumption toward active, creative stewardship of our own digital
lives.
