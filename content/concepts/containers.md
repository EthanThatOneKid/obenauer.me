[[containers]] serve as the fundamental, flexible vessels for [[items]] within a
[[graph]]. Unlike traditional, rigid file systems, they act as fluid
environments that support [[externalization]] of thought, allowing users to
group, nest, and rearrange digital [[items]] to mirror their mental models.

> [!idea] Containers transform the computer from a static filing cabinet into a
> malleable landscape for [[thinking]].

By prioritizing [[agency]] and [[interoperability]], these structures ensure
that information remains discoverable and actionable, enabling a seamless
transition between various [[views]] and [[context]]. They are the essential
building blocks for creating [[interfaces]], shifting the burden of
[[organization]] from the human to the environment itself.

> allows us to compose fluid, interconnected [[workspaces|workspaces]] where the relationship

> [[architecture]] fundamentally shifts how we interact with our digital

> open [[graph]] of [[items]]. By leveraging [[portability]], we can freely move
> and embed these [[items]], using [[transclusion]] to create unified [[views]]
> that reflect our personal [[workflow]] and [[thinking]] patterns.

> > [!idea] The core shift is from "[[apps]] as containers" to "all things as
