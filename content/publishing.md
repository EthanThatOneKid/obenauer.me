[[publishing]] represents the bridge between our local [[computing]] environments and the broader network, enabling us to share and import [[items]] fluidly across domains. By treating information as granular, [[composition]]-ready units, publishing moves beyond static files; it allows for the distribution of single [[assets]] or complex, recursive [[collections]]—such as feeds, blogs, or books—that others can incorporate directly into their own [[workspaces]]. 

> [!idea] The core shift is one of sovereignty and interoperability: the publisher defines the structure of the [[data]], but the subscriber retains the [[agency]] to filter, integrate, and recontextualize these published materials into their own personal systems.

This approach fundamentally reimagines the internet not as a collection of silos, but as a vast, interconnected [[graph]] of portable [[primitives]].
