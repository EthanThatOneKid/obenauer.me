[[logic]] represents the underlying structure and rationale governing our digital systems, ensuring that our [[computing]] environments reflect human [[thinking]] rather than arbitrary machine constraints. It is the invisible scaffolding behind [[architecture]], turning raw data into coherent, navigable experiences. By embedding sound logic into the way we handle [[item]], we move away from siloed applications toward a cohesive system where the relationship between information is as meaningful as the information itself.

> [!idea] Logic is not merely code; it is the intent made manifest.

When we refine the logic of our systems, we improve the transparency of our [[interaction]], enabling a more fluid [[discovery]] process. By prioritizing deep, intentional structure, we empower users to master their [[environment]] and reclaim their [[agency]].
