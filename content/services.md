# services

In our current [[computing]] landscape, services often manifest as opaque, remote entities that mediate our interactions with our own digital [[assets]]. By shifting away from this silos-first model, we move toward an [[architecture]] where the user retains core [[agency]] and [[context]]. True [[malleability]] requires that we stop viewing services as walled gardens and start seeing them as modular, interoperable threads that support our [[thinking]] and [[creativity]] rather than controlling the containers in which they reside.

> [!idea]
> We must transition from service-centric consumption to user-centric [[integration]], ensuring that the tools we use serve our [[intent]] rather than locking our data into specific, inaccessible domains.

When we disentangle the utility of a service from its proprietary boundary, we open the door to genuine [[discovery]] and [[evolution]] of our digital [[environment]]. The goal is not the service itself, but the [[value]] unlocked when data flows freely across the [[graph]] of our personal and professional lives.
