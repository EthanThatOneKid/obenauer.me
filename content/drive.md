title: drive
---

[[drive|drives]] represent the foundational mechanisms within our [[personal]] [[computing]] [[environment]] that bridge the gap between abstract [[automation]] and the concrete [[item|items]] we interact with daily. By decoupling data from the rigid, siloed [[interface|interfaces]] of traditional [[software]], drives allow us to treat information with true [[sovereignty]], enabling us to flow across disparate [[domain|domains]] without friction. This approach transforms our [[workspace|workspaces]] into fluid, user-defined [[environment|environments]] where every [[asset|assets]]—regardless of its origin—is a first-class citizen, fostering deeper [[thinking]] and more meaningful [[creation]].

> [!idea] The shift from "apps" to "[[item|items]]" mediated by services and drives is essential for achieving a truly malleable and integrated digital life.

> [!important] By moving away from fixed [[window|windows]] and toward a unified [[graph]], we reclaim control over our digital [[knowledge]], ensuring that our [[fact|facts]] remain a reflection of our own [[intent]] rather than the constraints of a provider.
