[[drive]] represent the foundational mechanisms within our personal [[computing]] environment that bridge the gap between abstract [[automation]] and the concrete [[item]] we interact with daily. By decoupling data from the rigid, siloed interfaces of traditional [[software]], drives allow us to treat information with true [[sovereignty]], enabling us to flow across disparate [[domain]] without friction. This approach transforms our [[workspace]] into fluid, user-defined environments where every [[asset|asset]]—regardless of its origin—is a first-class citizen, fostering deeper [[thinking]] and more meaningful [[creation]].

> [!idea] The shift from "apps" to "items" mediated by services and drives is essential for achieving a truly malleable and integrated digital life.

> [!important] By moving away from fixed windows and toward a unified [[graph]], we reclaim control over our digital [[knowledge]], ensuring that our [[fact]] remain a reflection of our own [[intent]] rather than the constraints of a provider.
