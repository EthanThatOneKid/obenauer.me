A ==log== is the granular, chronological record of the evolution of data. By treating the transformation of an [[item|item]] as a primary citizen—a piece of data itself—we move beyond static states to a living history of our digital work. This shifts our perspective toward [[mutation]]-aware systems where every action, change, and revision is captured, fostering greater [[evolvability]] and transparency within our [[workspace]].

> [!idea] By tracking these transformations, the operating system gains the capacity to reconstruct, audit, or branch the lifecycle of any information, effectively turning our [[workflow]] into a navigable, temporal narrative.

> [!note] Logs transform how we view our history: instead of losing our previous steps to a simple 'save' command, we maintain a rich, inspectable ledger of intent.
