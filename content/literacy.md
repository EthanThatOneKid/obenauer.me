# literacy

[[literacy]] represents the fundamental ability to fluently engage with the [[computing]] environments that serve as the substrate for our modern cognition. It is not merely a technical skill, but a shift in [[agency]] that allows us to move beyond passive consumption, enabling a deeper [[interaction]] with the digital tools that shape our thoughts. By developing a true literacy in our systems, we transcend the limitations of current [[interface]] design, turning our machines from adversarial barriers into harmonious extensions of our creative potential.

> [!important]
> ==True literacy is the capacity to mold our digital environments to serve human intent rather than conforming to the rigid defaults of software.==

> [!idea]
> When we understand the underlying structure of our tools—how information is itemized, linked, and organized—we reclaim the ability to shape our own intellectual landscape.
