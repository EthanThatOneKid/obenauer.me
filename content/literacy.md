# computer-literacy

Computer [[literacy|literacy]] is not merely the ability to operate software; it is the
==foundational capacity for users to understand, shape, and evolve their digital
environment==. True [[literacy|literacy]] requires moving beyond passive consumption toward
[[agency]], where the [[architecture|architecture]] of the system allows for [[malleability]]
that adapts to individual needs. By fostering an environment rooted in
[[evolvability]], we transform [[computing|computing]] from a rigid, opaque utility into an
extension of the self—a space where the user is an active participant in their
own digital reality rather than a subordinate to a pre-defined [[interface|interface]].

> [!idea] To achieve true [[literacy|literacy]], we must move away from fixed application
> silos and embrace [[composition]] that empower users to construct their own
> [[tools|tools]].

> [!note] [[literacy|Literacy]] is deeply tied to the visibility of the underlying data
> structures; when a system is inherently interpretable, the user gains the
> [[agency|agency]] to improve it.
