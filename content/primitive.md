A ==primitive== represents a foundational, well-defined interaction or building block that should be standardized across the OS to eliminate redundant labor. When we define these, we move away from rebuilding common behaviors—like [[undo]] or [[drag]]—and instead provide stable, higher-level abstractions that empower users and developers alike. By focusing on the [[item]] as a primary primitive, we unlock new ways of thinking about [[composition]], [[navigation]], and the underlying [[architecture]] of personal software.

> [!idea]
> Primitives are not just technical constraints; they are the vocabulary of interaction. When we settle these, we create the necessary surface area for true creativity and individual agency to thrive.

By unifying these base elements, we can build a more [[malleable]] environment that grows with us, fostering deeper [[literacy]] and enabling the fluid movement of thoughts across our digital tools.
