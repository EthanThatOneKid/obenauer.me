Axioms are the foundational [[principle|principles]] that anchor our [[design]]
and [[architecture]], serving as the immutable truths upon which we build our
[[system|systems]]. They are not merely rules, but the bedrock of [[logic]] that
guides how we bridge the gap between [[thinking]] and the machine. By embracing
these starting points, we ensure that our [[tool|tools]] remain
[[malleability|malleable]] and focused on true human [[intent]] rather than
arbitrary complexity.

> [!idea] We achieve true [[perfection]] not by piling on features, but by
> distilling our [[tool|tools]] down to these essential axioms—removing
> everything that does not support the core experience.

By grounding our work in clear [[axiom|axioms]], we resist the decay of
[[software]] and move toward a [[future]] of [[sovereignty]] in [[computing]],
ensuring that the [[system|systems]] we [[craft]] are as resilient as they are
intuitive.
