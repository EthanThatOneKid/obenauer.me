Axioms are the foundational [[principle]] that anchor our [[design]] and [[architecture]], serving as the immutable truths upon which we build our systems. They are not merely rules, but the bedrock of [[logic]] that guides how we bridge the gap between [[thinking]] and the machine. By embracing these starting points, we ensure that our tools remain [[malleability|malleable]] and focused on true human [[intent]] rather than arbitrary complexity.

> [!idea]
> We achieve true [[perfection]] not by piling on features, but by distilling our tools down to these essential axioms—removing everything that does not support the core experience.

By grounding our work in clear axioms, we resist the decay of software and move toward a future of [[sovereignty]] in [[computing]], ensuring that the systems we craft are as resilient as they are intuitive.
