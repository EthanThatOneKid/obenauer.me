To be [[personal]] is to reclaim the individual's role in the architecture of their own digital life. It is not merely a label, but a commitment to [[sovereignty]], where [[software]] serves the unique intent of the user rather than the extractive logic of platforms. By building systems that prioritize the individual—focusing on [[agency]], [[thinking]], and the seamless flow of thoughts—we move toward a future where our tools are extensions of our own minds, not barriers to them.
