To be **itemcentric** is to fundamentally shift our digital foundations, moving away from monolithic applications and toward the ==item as the core primitive== of our [[computing]] environments. By elevating the item, we unlock new dimensions of [[agency]] and [[creativity]], allowing information to flow freely across the boundaries that currently constrain our [[thinking]]. This approach transforms how we interact with data, replacing stagnant files with fluid, living structures that reflect the natural complexity of our work.

> [!idea] The core shift: By treating items as the primary unit of interaction, we replace the rigid application-first model with a modular, user-centric environment where content can be freely composed, referenced, and navigated without friction.
