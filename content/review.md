[[review]] is a vital mechanism for maintaining the flow of our digital lives, deeply connected to [[item]], [[context]], and [[thinking]]. By moving beyond the binary, high-stakes pressure of traditional inbox management, we transform the way we interact with our digital [[environment]]. Instead of treating tasks or messages as burdens to be purged, we utilize a ==spaced review== system to gracefully resurface ==active== but non-scheduled items—such as pending emails, saved links, or deferred tasks.

> [!idea] The power of the "skip"
> Traditional clearing actions like "delete" are often too heavy, leading to inbox stagnation. By enabling a lighter "skip for now" action, we lower the stakes. The system learns: if you skip often, it waits longer to bother you again, effectively shifting our relationship with information from reactive management to mindful stewardship.

This approach ensures that nothing is truly lost, only elegantly managed according to our current intent and capacity.
