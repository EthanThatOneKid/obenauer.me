[[portability]] challenges the status quo of modern [[computing]], where our
digital lives are confined to the rigid, siloed [[container|containers]] of
traditional applications. By breaking these walls, we move toward a vision where
individual [[item|items]]—be they [[note|notes]], [[task|tasks]], or images—are
no longer trapped within a single [[interface]], but can be freely moved and
composed across various [[environment|environments]]. This ==inversion of the
operating [[system]]== allows the [[system]] to utilize components provided by
our chosen [[software]], fostering a fluid space that truly reflects our unique
[[thinking]] and [[workflow|workflows]].

> [!idea] Universal data portability is the key to unlocking a more cohesive
> digital existence, transforming computers from islands of isolated
> [[tool|tools]] into a unified, flexible [[workspace]].

> [!note] We must stop [[thinking]] of programs as monolithic [[window|windows]]
> and start seeing them as providers of discrete, interchangeable [[view|views]]
> for our data.
