# collections

[[collections]] serve as the fluid, user-defined [[containers]] that transcend static [[hierarchy]], enabling us to gather [[items]] based on intent rather than rigid file paths. By treating data as malleable units, we break away from the [[silos]] of traditional [[os]] architecture, allowing information to live in multiple states simultaneously. This shift toward [[itemized]] organization empowers the user to cultivate a personal [[workspace]] that mirrors the way human [[cognition]] actually functions—not in folders, but in shifting, meaningful associations.

> [!idea]
> A collection is not a destination; it is a dynamic [[view]] of our digital [[assets]], shaped by our current [[workflow]] and ongoing [[curiosity]].

By leveraging [[metadata]] and [[linking]], we move beyond simple storage toward a living [[environment]] where our [[projects]] remain interconnected and constantly evolving. This is the essence of [[sovereignty]] in our digital lives: the ability to structure our own [[knowledge]] without the constraints of forced, singular categorizations.
