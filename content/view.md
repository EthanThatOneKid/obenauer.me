A [[view]] is the primary mechanism for interacting with the [[item]] as a core primitive. By decoupling data from rigid application silos, a view acts as a malleable lens, allowing the user to ==compose and reconstruct their digital environment== on demand. Because view definitions are themselves items, they are subject to the same principles of [[evolvability]], [[interoperability]], and [[sovereignty]] as any other piece of data, enabling users to fluidly swap, fold, or gesturally construct interfaces that align with their current [[intent]].

> [!idea]
> When we treat views as first-class, swappable components, the computer ceases to be a collection of closed programs and becomes a coherent, custom-fit [[workspace]].

> [!note]
> By moving beyond fixed application boundaries, we unlock the ability to zoom into the specific [[context]] of our work, treating the interface as a dynamic, user-defined projection of the underlying data graph.
