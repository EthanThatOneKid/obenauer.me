# local

[[local]] is the reclamation of the machine as a primary sovereign space, fundamentally intertwined with [[agency]], [[computing]], and [[ownership]]. By shifting the locus of data and interaction away from remote servers and back to the individual's hardware, we restore the speed, privacy, and resilience inherent to true [[personal]] control. This is not merely a technical configuration, but a design necessity for a healthier relationship between human and tool.

> [!important] The network should be an adjunct for synchronization, never the gatekeeper of intent or access.

When we operate locally, our digital [[environment]] becomes a responsive, private extension of our [[cognition]]. It allows for an uninterrupted [[timeline]] of work, where the system is always ready, always yours, and perpetually available to serve your [[creativity]] without external interference. ==True local-first systems belong to the user, not the provider.==

> [!idea] By anchoring software in the local, we transform it from a remote service we lease into a persistent artifact we possess.
