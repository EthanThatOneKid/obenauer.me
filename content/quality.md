If the destination is [[quality]], the journey is [[quantity]]. To build a
masterpiece—whether in [[shipping]], [[design]], or [[thinking]]—we must move
beyond dissecting the finished state and embrace the [[iteration]]. We often
mistake the ingredients of excellence (attention to detail, innovation) for the
[[process]] itself, but they cannot be combined to replicate a result. Instead,
true [[quality]] is found by producing, over and over again, allowing the sheer
volume of work to provide the lessons necessary for growth.

> [!note] Your masterpiece isn’t built by endless refinement of a single idea;
> it is built by the accumulation of a lifetime of work, where each piece serves
> as a necessary step in your own evolution.
