# ownership

[[sovereignty]] is the bedrock of our digital existence, inextricably linked to [[localfirst]] principles, the [[architecture]] of our [[os]], and the [[independence]] of our personal data. Modern software too often treats our life’s work like a tenant’s belongings, subject to the landlord's whims. True ownership shifts this power back to the individual, ensuring our information remains ==mutable, portable, and yours to command==. It is not merely a legal status; it is a technical imperative for human-centered [[computing]].

> [!note] The sovereign individual
> When you own your data, you are no longer a digital serf. You reclaim the right to navigate your own [[environment]] without corporate gatekeepers.

> [!idea] Future-proofing our tools
> By prioritizing [[local]] data storage, we ensure that our [[thinking]] and [[workflow]] remain resilient against the decay of remote services.
