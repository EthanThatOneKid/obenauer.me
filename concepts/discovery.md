# [[discovery]]

[[discovery]] is the act of contributing a new node into the [[graph]] of human
knowledge. It is not merely retrieval but an ==inquisitive process of
unfolding==, where we move beyond existing understanding to find clues that
answer fundamental questions. This pursuit is deeply linked to [[research]], as
it requires us to engage with the world—whether through software, nature, or
art—to find our own spark and refine our [[thinking]]. When we treat our efforts
as contributions to a collective, we move past passive consumption toward a more
meaningful, vision-oriented [[context]] for existence.

> [!idea] [[discovery]] is about finding the 'clues' that help us build a better
> vision of the future, transforming our relationship with the tools we use for
> work and thought.

> [!note] True [[discovery]] demands that we stop focusing on the surface and
> start looking for the substance that advances human understanding.
