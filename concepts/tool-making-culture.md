# tool-making-culture

Most modern [[computing]] environments relegate people to passive consumers. A
true [[tool-making-culture]] transcends this, fostering [[agency|user-agency]] and
reclaiming [[personal-computing]] from the rigid dictates of pre-packaged
software. It envisions a world where the ability to craft one's own instruments
is embedded into the fabric of [[interaction]], supported by
[[malleable-software]] that adapts to our specific, evolving needs.

> [!idea] The core shift We must stop merely inhabiting fixed digital buildings
> and start laying our own bricks. This is not about surface-level
> [[customization]]; it is about =fundamental creation=, moving beyond
> consumption to genuine cognitive augmentation.

By prioritizing this culture, we move toward a [[tool-making-culture]] that
treats the computer as a substrate for thought rather than an adversary,
ensuring we remain the architects of our own digital experiences.
