# fundamental-units

To reimagine personal [[computing]], we must identify its ==fundamental-units==.
By shifting our perspective toward an [[items]], we move away from monolithic
applications and toward a system where the '[[items]]' serves as the core
primitive. This atomic [[architecture]] is essential for fostering [[agency]]
and ensuring that our digital environments remain truly malleable.

> [!idea] When we treat the [[items]] as the fundamental unit, we unlock the
> potential for a more fluid relationship between our tools and our intent.

By centering our [[design]] on these small, interoperable pieces, we create a
foundation that supports [[malleability]] and complex information architectures,
ultimately allowing users to externalize their thoughts with greater clarity and
depth.
