A [[workspace]] is an [[items|item]] that acts as a container for other
[[items]], holding their references and state within a [[graph|graph-os]]. By treating
a workspace as a first-class entity—complete with its own metadata and
structure—we gain the ability to create non-volatile, heterogeneous collections
of information. This enables us to return to a specific line of work or train of
thought exactly where we left off. However, the true vision lies in creating
==fluid workspaces== that adapt to our needs, moving beyond static snapshots to
allow us to synthesize past findings into new, active inquiries.

> [!idea] Digital workspaces should be less rigid; they must support the way we
> naturally think, allowing us to pivot between continuity of detail and
> high-level summaries of past work.

See also: [[items|item]], [[paths|browsing-paths]], [[graph|graph-os]],
[[workspaces|fluid-workspaces]].
