> [!idea] **browsing-paths** represent a reimagined approach to navigation, moving away from rigid, app-bound windows toward a fluid, [[items|item-centric]] experience. By leveraging [[universal-reference-containers]], users can treat any entity—be it a granular note, a specific email, or a complex task—as an independent object that exists anywhere in the system.

> [!note] This shift allows for the creation of meaningful [[context|contexts]] where items are brought together based on their relationships rather than their host application. Instead of managing windows, we navigate through the [[graph]] of our own work, tracking our journey across these interconnected entities to support natural [[thinking]] and discovery.

> [!important] By moving toward [[malleable-software]], we replace the disjointed nature of current operating systems with a continuous, path-based exploration, ensuring that our digital environment reflects the true, non-linear structure of our thoughts.
