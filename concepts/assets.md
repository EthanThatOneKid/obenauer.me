In an [[items]], ==[[assets]]== are the foundational building blocks of the
digital environment—discrete, manageable units that exist independently of
specific applications. By shifting the focus from opaque, walled-garden files to
transparent [[items]], we can finally treat our digital information as a
cohesive [[graph]]. This transition empowers users to construct their own
[[views]], enabling richer [[relationships]] and seamless [[interaction]] with
the data that defines their daily lives.

> [!idea] [[assets]] represent the shift toward a more malleable [[computing]]
> future, where data is no longer trapped in monolithic containers but is
> instead available to be referenced, composed, and surfaced whenever and
> wherever it is needed to support human thought.

Ultimately, these [[assets]] are the raw material for our [[thinking]], and by
bringing them into a unified [[architecture]], we reclaim our [[agency]] over
the tools we use.
