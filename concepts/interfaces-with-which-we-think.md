[[interfaces-with-which-we-think]] are not the inherent laws of [[computing]], but rather fragile constructs of human imagination. Much like the twelve-month calendar, our current digital metaphors—[[apps]], [[windows]], and [[desktops]]—are simply manufactured abstractions designed to bridge the gap between human intent and binary [[logic]]. When these legacy patterns become rigid, they cease to facilitate our cognitive processes and instead become constraints that limit our potential. We must move toward more [[composable-systems]] that adapt to how we actually think, rather than forcing our thoughts into the rigid, siloed containers of the past.
