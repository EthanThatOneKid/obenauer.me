The concept of ==[[universal-reference-containers]]== posits that every digital entity—whether it be a note, task, event, or document—possesses the inherent capability to contain any other entity. By breaking away from the rigid silos of modern applications, this principle allows us to compose fluid, interconnected workspaces where the relationship between the container and the contained is dynamic and context-aware. This [[architecture]] fundamentally shifts how we interact with our digital environment, moving toward a system where information is no longer trapped in specific [[apps]] but is instead treated as a first-class participant in an open [[graph]] of [[items]]. By leveraging [[universal-data-portability]], we can freely move and embed these [[entities]], using [[transclusion]] to create unified [[views]] that reflect our personal [[workflow]] and [[thinking]] patterns.

> [!idea] The core shift is from "[[apps]] as containers" to "all things as containers." 

> [!note] How an [[items|item]] is displayed within another is defined by their [[interaction]], enabling a richer, more responsive [[computing]] experience.
