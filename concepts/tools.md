[[tools]] are not mere utilities; they are the ==interfaces with which we
think==, designed to deepen our engagement with our own cognitive processes. By
fostering [[agency]] and curiosity, these systems transition from passive tools
to active partners in our intellectual lives. They rely on a foundation of
[[malleability]] that allows individuals to reshape their digital environments,
turning the computer into an extension of the mind rather than a hurdle to its
expression.

> [!idea] When we treat the interface as an extension of the self, we move
> closer to a true [[symbiosis]].

By prioritizing an [[items]] structure, these tools facilitate fluid
[[externalization]] processes, where capturing a fleeting idea is as natural as
the thought itself. This shift requires us to rethink our [[computing]]
architectures, ensuring that the software remains as dynamic as the people using
it.
