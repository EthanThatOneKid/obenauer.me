[[navigation|Navigation]] is the foundational act of moving through our digital environments, reimagined as a fluid exploration of [[paths]], [[context|contexts]], and the underlying [[graph]] of our personal knowledge. Rather than rigid hierarchies, [[navigation|navigation]] relies on ==browsing active [[context|contexts]]== and tracking recent journeys, allowing us to maintain [[agency|agency]] as we move between [[items]] and their varied [[relationships]]. By prioritizing semantic movement over mechanical file traversal, we transform the [[interfaces|interface]] from a container into a living, responsive space.

> [!idea] 
> True [[navigation|navigation]] is not about finding a location, but about sustaining the flow of thought while shifting between different dimensions of data.

> [!note]
> Our digital tools should support our natural tendency to traverse information through [[association|association]] rather than strictly predefined [[paths|paths]].
