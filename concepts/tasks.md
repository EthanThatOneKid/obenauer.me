# [[tasks]]

[[tasks]] are not merely a list of obligations, but rather dynamic
==intentions== waiting to be woven into the fabric of our [[computing]]
environment. By treating [[tasks]] as first-class [[items]] within an
[[items|itemized-os]], we shift from passive management to active engagement,
allowing [[context]] to flow seamlessly between the work we do and the
information we hold.

> [!idea] When [[tasks]] are deeply integrated into the OS, they cease to be
> external burdens and instead become the natural focal points of our digital
> [[thinking]].

By leveraging [[transclusion]], a task can exist simultaneously in a project
roadmap, a meeting note, and the daily dashboard, ensuring our focus remains
anchored without the friction of duplication. We must move beyond fragmented
to-do lists and toward a fluid, interconnected [[graph]] of activity that truly
respects human [[user-agency]].
