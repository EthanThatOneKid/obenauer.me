> [!note] [[interaction|Interaction]]
> [[interaction|Interaction]] is the fundamental =dialogue between human intent and the computational environment=. It transcends mere input, functioning as the primary vehicle through which we manifest [[agency|agency]] and curiosity within our [[thinking]] processes. By engaging with [[items]] across fluid [[views]], we transform raw data into a dynamic extension of our cognitive [[architecture|architecture]].

To interact is to curate the [[context]] of our digital lives, ensuring that software remains a malleable collaborator rather than a rigid set of instructions. True [[interaction|interaction]] bridges the gap between fragmented data and cohesive meaning, allowing us to navigate our [[relationships]] and digital landscapes with purpose. When we rethink the interfaces we use, we are really questioning how we construct the very containers for our [[memory|memory]] and [[discovery|discovery]].
