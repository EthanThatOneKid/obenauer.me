# [[rendering]]

[[rendering|Rendering]] is the ==act of manifesting structured data into a
tactile, cognitive space==, bridging the gap between raw [[items]] and human
[[thinking]]. It is not merely a visual process but a fundamental
[[interfaces|interface]] strategy where we define how information is surfaced
and experienced across diverse [[paths]]. By decoupling the underlying
[[representation]] from its display, we allow for dynamic [[transclusion]]—where
content adapts to the user's immediate needs rather than remaining trapped in
static, rigid applications. We must prioritize fluid interfaces that respect the
user's [[agency]], ensuring that every [[rendering]] is a responsive
conversation between the human and their digital environment.

> [!idea] [[rendering|Rendering]] is the bridge from data to insight.

> [!note] True [[computing]] interfaces should be malleable, allowing us to
> change how we see our world on the fly.
