# [[memory]]

Digital [[memory]] is not merely a storage capacity; it is an extension of our
own [[thinking]] process, allowing us to [[externalization|externalize]] our
cognition to expand our capacity for complexity. By integrating our [[items]]
into a cohesive [[graph]], we move beyond simple file management into a state of
active recall and reflection. This requires us to [[design]] systems that honor
the human tendency to favor [[paths]] of least resistance, ensuring our tools
support our natural patterns of work rather than imposing rigid, artificial
structures.

> [!idea] We push much of our [[thinking]] out into our computers so that we can
> think about more things. [[memory|Memory]] in this [[context]] acts as a
> bridge between the transient moment and our broader body of work.

> [!note] When we rely on rigid folder structures, we fight our own cognitive
> workflows. True digital [[memory]] should allow for fluid [[views]] and
> [[transclusion]] to re-contextualize our past work in the present.
