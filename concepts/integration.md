# [[integration|integration]]

[[integration]] is the dissolution of boundaries between our [[thinking]], our [[computing]], and our [[context]]. Rather than siloed applications, we seek a fluid environment where [[assets]] flow freely across the [[graph]], enabling a cohesive digital [[architecture|architecture]] that mirrors the organic nature of human cognition. 

> [!idea] 
> True [[integration|integration]] is not mere [[interoperability|interoperability]]; it is the seamless ==unification of intent and medium==. 

By moving beyond the document-centric model, we enable [[transclusion]] as a standard, ensuring that information maintains its integrity even when manifested in multiple [[views]]. This creates a persistent, responsive state where the computer acts as a true extension of the mind, bridging the gap between raw data and meaningful action.
