# [[views]]

[[views|Views]] represent a profound shift in how we interact with our digital
tools, moving away from rigid application silos toward a fluid, user-defined
[[computing]] environment. By decoupling the [[interfaces|interface]] from the
data, [[views]] allow us to interact with [[items]] through specialized lenses
that can be swapped, created, or shared to serve specific human needs. Because
view definitions are themselves [[items]], the system becomes recursively
malleable, enabling us to build even better tools for modifying those very
definitions—a core tenet of enabling deep [[thinking]] and [[agency]].

> [!idea] A meta-UI language By treating [[views]] as a shared specification, we
> can achieve [[interfaces|interface]] consistency across entirely different
> software stacks and platforms, ensuring our environment respects the user's
> intent over the developer's rigid constraints.

> [!note] Interconnected reality When we decouple presentation, we open the
> possibility of [[transclusion]]—bringing disparate pieces of information
> together into a single, cohesive workspace that evolves as our [[paths]]
> through information change. We move from static documents to dynamic, lived-in
> digital spaces where every view is an invitation to reshape our relationship
> with the machine.

> [[views|user-created-views]] represent a fundamental shift in the
> [[symbiosis|human-computer-relationship]], moving away from
> rigid, pre-packaged software toward a landscape where individuals define how
> they interact with their digital world. By leveraging [[apps|atomized-apps]] and
> [[items|item-based-computing]], users can construct their own interfaces for any
> data type—emails, notes, or [[tasks]]—ensuring that information is not trapped
> in silos but is instead accessible via [[unified-views]].
> 
> > [!idea] Empowerment through [[customization]] We must stop [[thinking]] of
> > computers as tools we use, and start seeing them as environments we inhabit.
> > When a user can craft a view to suit their specific intent, the computer
> > transitions from a barrier into an extension of thought.
> 
> This approach relies on [[portability|universal-data-portability]] and the ability to
> compose workflows through [[gestures|gestural-view-construction]]. By treating the
> [[items|item]] as the core primitive, we enable an ecosystem where
> [[views|swappable-views]] allow for infinite experimentation with how we organize and
> perceive our work.
