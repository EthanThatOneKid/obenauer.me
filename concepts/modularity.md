A module is the fundamental unit of [[composition]] in an itemized system, weaving together [[items]], [[views]], and [[actions]] into a functional whole. By bundling these components with [[automation]], we transform static data into dynamic, interactive experiences that can be published to the internet, allowing others to engage directly with our systems. 

> [!idea] 
> [[modularity|Modularity]] is the bridge between personal [[organization]] and public [[interaction]]; it is how we share not just information, but the very capability to act upon it.

Ultimately, this approach shifts the paradigm of software from rigid applications to ==composed, behavior-rich modules== that reflect our personal intent and [[context]].
