# timeline-of-use

The [[timeline-of-use]] is a profound reorientation of our digital existence, moving beyond the artificial fragmentation of [[apps]] and [[windows]] toward a unified, continuous stream of all our computational interactions. It envisions a world where every input, modification, and moment of engagement is inscribed into an unbroken, chronological record, inherently supporting [[memory]] and [[context]]. This continuous ledger provides an unparalleled substrate for a symbiotic human-computer partnership, allowing us to traverse the evolving states of our work and revisit specific moments with ease.

> [!idea] 
> By recording our digital lives as a fluid, historical sequence, we transform the computer from a collection of static files into a living extension of our own cognitive history.

==The timeline-of-use liberates us from the rigidities of current systems==, empowering a fluid, historically rich interaction with our digital [[assets]] and [[fluid-workspaces]].
