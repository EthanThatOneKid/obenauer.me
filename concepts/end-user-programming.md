[[end-user-programming]] is the bridge between mere usage and true [[agency|agency]], transforming the computer from a static appliance into a malleable environment. By collapsing the distinction between changing data and defining [[views]], we enable individuals to reshape their digital [[workspaces]] as naturally as they might rearrange physical furniture. This is the [[evolution|evolution]] of [[computing]] where the software reflects the user’s [[thinking]] rather than the constraints of a developer's predetermined [[interfaces|interface]].

> [!idea] Essence
> True [[user-agency]] requires the power to reconfigure, remix, and redesign our tools to match our unique, unfolding needs. When we treat [[interfaces|interface]] [[composition|composition]] as a personal act of [[malleable-software]] creation, we move closer to the ideal of the personal computer.

Ultimately, this is about moving away from being a tenant in someone else’s software toward becoming an architect of our own digital lives.
