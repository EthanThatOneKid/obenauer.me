# [[interoperability]]

[[interoperability]] is the antitoxin to the digital silos that currently
imprison our thoughts. It is the fundamental ability for systems and data to
talk and collaborate, serving as a core enabler for a [[computing]] environment.
When tools interoperate, data becomes a fluid resource that can transform and
combine across applications without friction, deeply supporting [[augmentation]]
and the creation of [[workspaces]].

> [!note] The user, not the vendor, must dictate the flow of information.

By moving away from isolated, rigid [[apps]] toward a composable, intelligent
ecosystem, we enable a truly [[local]] experience. This shift allows for
[[programming]] where your digital environment can be constructed and evolved to
meet your specific needs, ensuring that your work is not locked away, but
remains accessible and malleable.
