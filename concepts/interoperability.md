# [[interoperability]]

[[interoperability]] is the antitoxin to the digital silos that currently
imprison our thoughts. It is the fundamental ability for systems and data to
talk and collaborate, serving as a core enabler for a
[[computing|personal-computing-redefined]] environment. When tools
interoperate, data becomes a fluid resource that can transform and combine
across applications without friction, deeply supporting
[[augmentation|cognitive-augmentation]] and the creation of [[workspaces|fluid-workspaces]].

> [!note] The user, not the vendor, must dictate the flow of information.

By moving away from isolated, rigid [[apps]] toward a composable, intelligent
ecosystem, we enable a truly [[local|local-first-software]] experience. This shift
allows for [[programming|end-user-programming]] where your digital environment can be
constructed and evolved to meet your specific needs, ensuring that your work is
not locked away, but remains accessible and malleable.
